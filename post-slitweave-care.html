How to wash a slitweave without wrecking it

A slitweave is a lattice of slits cut by hand and woven back together. That is what makes it move like a second skin, and it is also what makes it fussy in the wash. Treat it like a normal legging and you will stretch the weave out or fray the cut edges. Treat it right and it holds its shape set after set.

Here is the whole routine. It takes about five minutes.

The five-minute wash

  1. Turn it inside out. The cut edges of the weave face in, so they cannot catch on anything.
  2. Hand wash cold. Cold water, one drop of mild detergent, a gentle swish for a minute. Hot water is the enemy, it kills the four-way stretch.
  3. Press, do not wring. Squeeze the water out between your hands or a towel. Wringing twists the slits out of line.
  4. Lay flat to dry. Flat on a towel, out of direct sun. While it is still damp, run your hands down the weave to set it back into shape.
  5. Store it loose. Flat or loosely rolled, ties tucked in. Never crammed in a bag with hardware that can snag it.

What to never do

No machine wash, no dryer, no wringing, no hot water, no direct sun for hours. Every one of those pulls the weave out of true or breaks down the stretch. A slitweave is hand-made and one of a kind, so there is no returning a shrunk one. Five minutes of care is the trade.

Metal ties come off before the wash if yours are removable. Cloth ties stay on and wash fine. Either way, dry them flat with the piece so nothing warps.
