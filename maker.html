THE MAKER

Made by one pair of hands.

Firedancing is a solo studio. Every catsuit and bodysuit is cut, woven and finished by the founder, a fire performer who got tired of stagewear that could not take a real set. No factory. No production line. No two pieces exactly alike.

It started on stage. Spins, drops, floor work and fire ask a lot of what you wear. Off-the-rack pieces snagged, slid, or fell apart by the encore. So the answer became: cut it yourself, weave it to hold, and build it around the way a performer actually moves.

Every slitweave is hand-cut. A lattice of slits opened by hand and woven back together down the front and sides, on a four-way-stretch base that moves like skin and holds its shape. That is slow work, and it is the whole point. When you order, you are buying the art and the hours behind it, not a print run.

Two ways to get one. Grab a finished piece off the rack, or build one laced to your exact fit, made to order in about two to four weeks. Same craft either way. Because every piece is one of a kind, all sales are final, so check the size chart and reach out if you are between sizes.
