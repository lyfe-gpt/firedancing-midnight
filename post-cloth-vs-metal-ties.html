Cloth vs metal ties: which to pick

Every slitweave is finished by hand where the weave laces together, and you get to choose what laces it: soft cloth ties or metal hardware. They look different, they feel different on skin, and they read differently from the crowd. Here is how to pick.

Cloth ties

Soft, quiet, and forgiving. Cloth ties sit flat against the skin, so they disappear into the piece and never catch cold under stage lights. They are the easy pick for floor work and long sets where hardware would dig in. They also wash right along with the piece, no removal needed. If you want the cut itself to be the whole story, go cloth.

Metal ties

Hardware and shine. Metal ties throw light. Under a spotlight or against fire they catch and flash on every spin, which reads hard from the back row. They add weight and a little swing to the lacing, which some performers love for movement. The trade is comfort: metal can press on a long floor sequence, and you take it off before you wash. If your act is about drama and glint, go metal.

The quick rule

Close-up, floor-heavy, comfort-first set? Cloth. Big stage, fire, spotlight, and you want to be seen from distance? Metal. On a made-to-order build you choose ties as part of the fit, so you can match them to the act instead of the other way around.
